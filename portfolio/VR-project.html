VR for Acrophobia Treatment

BSc IT Final Project - Exploring VR therapy solutions

Demo of the VR acrophobia treatment experience

Project Overview

This project was developed as my final year project for my BSc in Information Technology. The goal was to explore whether virtual reality could be effectively used as a therapeutic tool to help treat acrophobia (fear of heights).

Using the Oculus Meta Quest 2 and Unity 3D, I created a series of immersive environments designed to gradually expose users to heights in a controlled manner. The project involved extensive research into clinical exposure therapy techniques and user experience design.

The VR experience includes three progressively challenging scenarios: a rope bridge, a small building with a log walkway, and a tall skyscraper. Participants were guided through these environments while their anxiety levels were monitored using pre- and post-experience questionnaires.

The system includes physiological monitoring to track the user's anxiety levels throughout the experience.

Development Process

Research Phase

Conducted literature review on VR therapy and acrophobia treatment. Developed two specialized questionnaires: Acrophobia Questionnaire (AQ) and Height Interaction Questionnaire (HIQ) to assess participant fear levels.

Environment Design

Created three immersive scenarios using Unity and assets from the Asset Store:

  • Rope bridge between rock formations
  • Small building with log walkway
  • Tall skyscraper environment

Implementation

Developed VR interaction systems including:

  • XR Origin setup for VR movement
  • Custom Hand Presence scripts
  • Environment physics and collision systems
  • Participant consent and debriefing systems

Testing & Evaluation

Conducted controlled trials with 8 participants (4 with acrophobia, 4 without) using pre- and post-experience questionnaires to measure anxiety reduction.

Research Findings

Effectiveness

75% of acrophobic participants showed measurable reduction in anxiety symptoms after VR exposure sessions

Limitations

Study identified need for more realistic environmental factors (wind, balance challenges) in future iterations

User Feedback

Participants reported the experience was "surprisingly effective" and "felt realistic but safe"

Future Work

Plan to enhance realism with additional environmental factors and expand testing to larger groups

Project Outcomes

  • Successfully demonstrated VR's potential as an acrophobia treatment tool
  • Developed complete VR therapy prototype with multiple environments
  • Documented research methodology and results in comprehensive paper
  • Identified areas for future improvement in VR therapeutic applications
