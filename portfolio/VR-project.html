VR for Acrophobia Treatment

BSc IT Final Project - Exploring VR therapy solutions

Project Overview

This project was developed as my final year project for my BSc in Information Technology. The goal was to explore whether virtual reality could be effectively used as a therapeutic tool to help treat acrophobia (fear of heights).

The VR experience gradually exposes users to increasing heights in a controlled environment, allowing them to confront their fear safely. The system includes physiological monitoring to track the user's anxiety levels throughout the experience.

Technical Details

  • Developed using Unity 3D game engine
  • Oculus Rift for VR immersion
  • Implemented gradual exposure therapy techniques
  • Integrated biometric feedback (heart rate monitoring)
  • Custom shaders for realistic height perception
  • Dynamic difficulty adjustment based on user responses

Development Process

The project began with extensive research into both VR technology and the psychology of phobias. I collaborated with psychology students to design therapeutic scenarios that would be both effective and safe for users.

After prototyping several environments, I conducted user testing with volunteers who self-identified as having some degree of acrophobia. Their feedback helped refine the experience to maximize therapeutic benefit while minimizing discomfort.

Results

The project showed promising results in initial testing with participants reporting reduced anxiety levels after multiple VR exposure sessions. The research paper was awarded a distinction by the university.
